Bearing Witness: Portraits of Trees in the Reichsparteitagsgelände

Reichsparteitagsgelände, the Nazis party rally grounds, occupied 2718 acres of land in the southeastern section of the city of Nuremberg, Germany. The site hosted six party rallies in the 1930s, but its intended use as the legislative/congressional center of the Nazi party never materialized.

Today, the site hosts the Documentation Center, a museum dedicated to educating visitors about the forces behind the Nazi party and the consequences of facism. Also remaining are the vast walkable grounds, which today serve as sites for music festivals, holiday carnivals, and general recreational use to the public.

In 2018, after touring the site, I created six drawings of different trees I observed throughout the grounds. Immobile by nature and with decades of history embedded in their rings, trees offer an apt metaphor for witness. In their cross sections, trees reveal to us the inflection points and disturbances of their environmental history, and show how these events reverberate across the remaining trajectory of their growth.
